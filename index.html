Timeline

This is my story

  • May 2016

    LEARN Academy

    During my time in Product Support, I learned about Programming Bootcamps and eagerly researched the local bootcamps in San Diego. My interview with Rob at LEARN Academy quickly cemented my interest in attending a bootcamp, and it has been one of the most fulfilling decisions of my life.

  • November 2015

    Alere Product Support

    One year after entering the Process Analyst role, Alere downsized the manufacturing department, and I was moved to the Product Support Team. In that role, I used my previous experiences as an Analyst and QC Lab Tech to perform investigation of product failures. I also used my JMP and SQL skills to build tools to streamline many of the data trending and team performance processes that my team lead and department manager had been doing manually. I also created a small suite of tools for use during investigations however, being the sole investigator on my product team allowed me to avoid building a custom interface as I was the only person using these tools.

  • December 2014

    Alere Analyst Team

    After concluding my Fall classes at Palomar, I pursued further professional growth to make the money needed to fund a Master's Degree in Programming. I pitched my skills to the managers of the Process Analysts at Alere, and became a part of their team. As an Analyst, I had access to the company's MS SQL database and JMP, an analysis software with a scripting language. I self-taught SQL and JMP Script, and soon built tools to automate the data manipulations which were manually performed at the time. Eventually, I designed simple interfaces for those tools and distributed them to my colleagues, effectively standardizing some of the data analysis performed by individual team members.

  • January 2014

    Palomar College

    When I had saved up a sufficient amount of money, I decided to pursue an old curiosity of mine via online classes. I was always interested in learning how to program, but I never formally pursued it. One Python class was all it took for me to dive further into the world of programming and I ended up taking classes in Java and C. It was then that I knew that I wanted to pursue programming as a career.

  • January 2013

    Alere QC Lab

    Less than a month after my graduation, I was offered a job as a QC Lab Technician at Alere, a local biotech company, which I happily accepted. As part of a manufacturing support team, I was able to fully develop the communication skills required to operate in a team environment. However, I did not feel that I was being sufficiently challenged, and I sought growth in other areas.

  • 2006

    San Diego State University

    During my time at SDSU, I switched between a variety of science majors until I landed an internship as a research assistant to a Microbiology Masters. I was so enamored by the subject, that I loaded up on related courses and finally graduated with a BS in Biology. At the time, I didn't realize that it was my affinity for information and logic that drew me to the areas of Biology concerning DNA and inheritance.

About

I also love dogs

I am an aspiring Full Stack Developer with 3.5 years of experience in the Medical Device industry. My on the job experience building SQL database queries, writing JMP scripting tools to efficiently manipulate data, and designing simple interfaces to reduce the turnaround time of routine procedures has sparked my interest in computer programming. I am currently attending LEARN Academy's programming boot camp in pursuit of a career in software/web development in the Tech or Biotech industries.
